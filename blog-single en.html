History of the capital of Georgia, Tbilisi

  • Sara
  • Jan 1, 2020
  • 12 Comment

Tbilisi, the capital of Georgia, is a city with a long history and diverse culture that combines ancient antiquity and modern life. The city is located in the center of the country on the banks of the Kura River and is considered an important center of culture, trade and tourism. Here's an overview of Tbilisi:

History and culture: The history of Tbilisi dates back to the fifth century BC, and the city has witnessed multiple historical stages that left their cultural mark on many historical monuments and buildings such as Narikala Castle and Freedom Square. Tbilisi is characterized by unparalleled cultural diversity, where traditional Georgian heritage coexists with modern and international aspects.

Tbilisi is home to many famous tourist attractions, such as the New Sultan David Bridge, the large Orthodox Cathedral, and Freedom Square, which is considered a vital center for the social and commercial life of the city.

the amazing nature: Tbilisi is surrounded by stunning nature, including the Caucasus mountain ranges and the Black Sea region, providing great opportunities for outdoor adventures and enjoyment of stunning nature and environmental diversity.

Delicious foods:

Georgia is famous for its rich traditions and delicious and diverse cuisine that reflects the diversity of its cultures and ancient history. Here are some of the most famous dishes in Georgia:

  • Jouri bread (Georgian Bread): Bread is an essential part of eating in Georgia, and Georgian bread is famous for its great taste and texture. It is usually served with main dishes Or used as an accompaniment to dishes.
  • Grilled Vegetables (Mkhali): It is a popular dish in Georgia and consists of various vegetables such as spinach, eggplant, radishes, etc. It is cooked and squeezed, then shaped into balls and decorated with nuts, oil, and spices.
  • Khmer (Khachapuri): It is a type of dough stuffed with cheese, and khmer is one of the most famous Georgian dishes. It can be served in different forms, such as adigeni and imroni.
  • Quinoaji (Khinkali): Quinoaji is a popular dish in Georgia. It is a thin dough filled with meat or cheese filling and steamed.
  • Pickles (Marinated Vegetables): Georgia is famous for preparing various pickles that are an integral part of eating traditional Georgian food. Pickles include radishes, eggplant, green peppers, and cucumbers.
